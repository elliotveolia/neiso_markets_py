1. Spreads by Zone (spreads_[ZoneName].html)

    What it shows: The difference between RT and DA demand/prices for each zone over time
    What it tells you:
        If spread is positive: RT demand is higher than DA forecast (unexpected demand spike)
        If spread is negative: RT demand is lower than DA forecast (demand was overestimated)
        Large swings = harder to forecast, more uncertainty
        Patterns = predictable forecast errors (e.g., always higher in morning)

2. Average Spreads by Zone (average_spreads_by_zone.html)

    What it shows: Which zones have the biggest RT vs DA differences on average
    What it tells you:
        High positive average: That zone consistently has higher RT demand than forecasted
        High negative average: That zone consistently has lower RT demand than forecasted
        Zones with large spreads = harder to forecast, more volatile, likely higher RT prices
        This helps identify which zones are most unpredictable

3. Volatility Comparison (volatility_comparison.html)

    What it shows: How much RT prices swing vs DA prices for each zone
    What it tells you:
        RT volatility > DA volatility: RT market is more unpredictable/reactive
        Zones with high RT volatility = more price risk, harder to manage
        Why it matters: Higher volatility = higher prices (to compensate for risk)

4. Spreads by Hour of Day (spreads_by_hour.html)

    What it shows: When during the day RT vs DA differences are biggest
    What it tells you:
        Morning/evening peaks: Demand harder to forecast at peak times
        Night valleys: More predictable, smaller spreads
        Patterns = you can predict when RT prices will diverge most from DA
        This helps identify when to hedge or adjust operations
